Answered question
What's the best way to prepare for a DSA interview as a 6th semester student with no prior practice?
Accepted Answer
Start with arrays and strings — they form the backbone of at least 40% of interview questions at the tier of companies that recruit from campuses like ours. Don't try to do all 500+ LeetCode questions. Instead, pick one curated list (NeetCode 150 is solid) and do it twice. The second pass is where you actually internalize patterns. Pair this with explaining your approach out loud as you code — most rejections at the phone screen happen because candidates can't communicate their thinking, not because they got the wrong answer.
Answered question
Is React or Vue.js better to learn first for someone who knows basic JavaScript?
React, without hesitation — not because it's objectively better, but because it has far more job listings, a larger community, and most of the alumni here who are in frontend roles are using it. Learning React also forces you to genuinely understand JavaScript (closures, event loop, async/await) in a way that Vue's magic tends to abstract away. Once you know React well, picking up Vue or Angular takes a week.
