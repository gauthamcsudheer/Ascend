# Ascend — Brand & Voice Guidelines

**Companion to PRD v1.0**

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Version | 1.0 |
| Audience | Designers, copywriters, engineers writing UI strings, admins crafting communications |
| Purpose | A consistent voice and visual identity for Ascend |

---

## 1. Brand Essence

### 1.1 What Ascend Is
Ascend is the trusted knowledge-and-mentorship platform for the RSET community. It exists because the institutional knowledge of RSET — the experiences, the answers, the mentorship — deserves a permanent, accessible home where it can flow naturally between those who have walked the path and those who are starting it.

### 1.2 What Ascend Is Not
- Not LinkedIn — not for personal branding outside RSET
- Not Stack Overflow — not just for technical Q&A
- Not WhatsApp — not for transient chat
- Not a social network — not for engagement-as-an-end
- Not a job board — not for recruiting

### 1.3 The Promise
Every contribution earns its weight through verified seniority. Every voice is a real voice from the RSET community. Every interaction is built on institutional trust.

---

## 2. Brand Personality

Ascend's personality is best understood as a balanced contradiction: warm but grounded, ambitious but humble, modern but rooted.

### 2.1 Five Adjectives
- **Trusted** — Authority earned through verification, not algorithms
- **Earnest** — We mean what we say; we don't sell or hype
- **Quietly confident** — We know what we're for; we don't need to shout
- **Communal** — Belonging to the RSET family is the foundation
- **Useful** — Practical value over performative engagement

### 2.2 What Ascend Sounds Like
Imagine a senior student helping a junior. Or an alumnus speaking with a current student over coffee. Direct. Helpful. Without ceremony but with care. Not corporate, not casual to the point of careless.

### 2.3 What Ascend Doesn't Sound Like
- Tech-bro enthusiasm ("Game-changing platform!" — no)
- Corporate mush ("Synergistic ecosystem of stakeholders" — no)
- Excessive familiarity ("Hey buddy! Welcome aboard!" — no)
- Stiff formality ("Please be advised that the system requires verification" — no)

## 3. Visual Identity

### 3.1 Colors

The primary palette is **Maroon and Gold** — colors deeply tied to RSET's institutional identity. The palette extends with neutrals and functional colors for usability.

**Primary:**
- **Maroon (Primary):** A deep, dignified maroon — used for primary actions, headers, brand elements. The exact hex to be confirmed against RSET's official institutional palette during design phase.
- **Gold (Accent):** A warm, refined gold — used for highlights, badges, accents. Not flashy.

**Secondary / Neutrals:**
- **Off-white background** — Soft, easy on the eyes for long reading
- **Charcoal text** — Strong contrast for readability without harsh black
- **Neutral grays** — Multiple shades for subtle hierarchy (borders, secondary text, disabled states)

**Functional:**
- **Success green** — Subtle, for positive confirmations
- **Warning amber** — For warnings and pending states
- **Error red** — Used sparingly, only for genuine errors

**Color usage principles:**
- Maroon and Gold as accents; not dominating every screen
- High contrast for accessibility (WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1 for text)
- Functional colors used purposefully, not decoratively

### 3.2 Typography

**Recommended principles** (specific font families to be selected during design phase):
- **Headings:** A modern serif or grotesque sans — something with character that feels institutional without feeling old-fashioned
- **Body:** A highly legible sans-serif optimized for screen reading
- **Code/technical:** A monospace font for code blocks, IDs, technical content

**Type scale:**
- Page headings: large, confident
- Section headings: clear hierarchy
- Body: comfortable for long-form reading (16px+ base)
- Captions/metadata: smaller but still legible

### 3.3 Iconography
- Clean line icons; consistent stroke weight
- Persona icons for user avatars: distinctive shapes for Student / Faculty / Alumnus
- Badge icons: distinctive, recognizable at small sizes; Maroon and Gold tier colors

### 3.4 Layout Principles
- Generous whitespace — content breathes
- Clear hierarchy — important things look important
- Mobile-first responsive — nothing requires desktop
- Density appropriate to content type:
  - Q&A research: information-dense on desktop
  - Feed scrolling: looser on mobile
  - Profile and settings: clean and uncluttered

## 4. Voice & Tone

### 4.1 Voice (Always Consistent)

The voice is **knowledgeable, warm, and grounded**. It speaks plainly, respects the reader's intelligence, and never wastes their time.

**Voice principles:**
- **Use simple words.** Choose "use" over "utilize," "help" over "facilitate."
- **Speak in second person.** "You" and "your" — not "users" or "the user."
- **Be direct.** Get to the point in the first sentence.
- **Acknowledge real life.** "Verification typically takes 3 days" not "Verification will be completed within standard processing windows."
- **Avoid clichés.** No "rockstar contributors," no "leverage your network," no "join the conversation."

### 4.2 Tone (Adapts to Context)

Tone shifts depending on the moment:

**Welcoming (signup, first-time tour):** Warm but not gushing. *"Welcome to Ascend. Tell us a bit about yourself so we can connect you with the right people."*

**Helpful (empty states, prompts):** Encouraging without being pushy. *"No questions yet in this tag. Be the first to ask."*

**Reassuring (verification pending):** Clear and respectful. *"Your alumni account is being verified. We typically respond within 3 days. We'll notify you the moment you're approved."*

**Serious (moderation actions):** Firm and respectful, never harsh. *"Your post was removed for violating the community guideline against personal attacks. The original content is no longer visible."*

**Celebratory (badge earned, accept-notification):** Genuine without exaggeration. *"You earned the Helpful badge — 10 of your answers have been accepted."*

**Apologetic (errors, downtime):** Honest and brief. *"Something went wrong loading this page. We're looking into it. Please try again in a moment."*

### 4.3 Words We Use
- *Community*, not "user base"
- *Member*, not "user" (when context allows)
- *Question*, *answer*, *post* — not "content" generically
- *Reputation*, *Expertise Score* — our specific terms
- *Verified Alumnus*, *Faculty*, *Student* — capitalize personas when used as titles
- *Connect with*, *reach out to* — for connections
- *Mentor*, *guide*, *help* — for what alumni do

### 4.4 Words We Avoid
- *Engage* (vague tech-speak)
- *Leverage* (corporate)
- *Drive* (as in "drive engagement")
- *Followers* (LinkedIn/Twitter feel; we say "people who follow you")
- *Karma*, *upvotes-as-currency* (we have rep, but it's about expertise, not points)
- *Expert* without context (we say "Subject Specialist in [topic]" or "Verified Alumnus")
- *Onboard* as a verb ("get you onboarded" — no, "help you get started")

### 4.5 Punctuation & Style
- Sentence case for headings and buttons (not Title Case)
- Em dashes used naturally — they're fine
- Oxford commas where they help clarity
- No exclamation marks except in genuinely celebratory moments (badge earned, welcome) — never in errors or warnings
- Avoid emojis in core UI; permitted in user-generated content

## 5. Naming Conventions

### 5.1 Feature Names
Names are descriptive and clear, not cute. They tell users what the thing does.

| Feature | Display Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Q&A surface | "Questions" or "Q&A" | Both fine; pick one and use consistently |
| Experience Feed | "Experience" or "Feed" | "Feed" is more conversational |
| Resource Library | "Resources" | Singular and clear |
| Connection request system | "Connections" | Active members are "your connections" |
| Direct messaging | "Messages" | Standard term |
| Reputation | "Reputation" or "Expertise Score" | The number is rep; the public concept is Expertise Score |
| Mentorship Handshake (vision doc term) | (Removed in final design) | We don't use this term — it was never built as a feature |

### 5.2 Persona Names
Always capitalize when used as titles. Lowercase in regular prose.
- "She is an Alumnus" — uppercase as a title
- "alumni from across batches" — lowercase in prose

### 5.3 Badge Names
Badges have evocative, brief names. They're not literal descriptions.

| Badge | Name | Why this name |
|---|---|---|
| 10 accepted answers | Helpful | The recognition for being useful |
| 5 accepted connections | Connector | Active in building bridges |
| 5 endorsed resources | Curator | Quality contributor of useful links |
| Experience post crosses 50 upvotes | Storyteller | Narrative impact |
| 20 answers to early-sem students | Welcomer | Intentionally helping juniors |
| 80% acceptance rate over 25+ | Reliable | Trustworthy contribution |
| Tag-specific expertise | Subject Specialist | Specific expertise |
| 1000+ rep | Sage | Highest tier of accumulated wisdom |
| 100 accepted answers | Pillar | Consistent foundational contributor |
| 25 accepted connections | Open Door | Welcoming to many |
| Active 4+ semesters | Elder | Long-term presence |
| Top post in semester | Catalyst | Spark that drove conversation |

Avoid changing badge names without strong reason — they become part of the platform's identity.

### 5.4 Status Labels
- Verification: "Verified" / "Pending" / "Rejected"
- Resources: "Library" / "Pending" (the tab names)
- Questions: "Open" / "Archived"
- DM threads: "Active" / "Read-only"

## 6. Microcopy Patterns

### 6.1 Empty States
Empty states are an opportunity, not a failure. They guide.

**Bad:** "No questions found."
**Good:** "No questions yet in this tag. Be the first to ask."

**Bad:** "Your inbox is empty."
**Good:** "No connection requests right now. Check back later, or reach out to someone yourself."

### 6.2 Confirmation Messages
Confirm what happened in plain language; what to expect next.

**Bad:** "Action successful."
**Good:** "Your question is posted. We'll notify you when someone answers."

**Bad:** "Settings saved."
**Good:** "Saved. Your notification preferences will take effect from your next session."

### 6.3 Error Messages
Don't blame the user. Tell them what to do.

**Bad:** "Invalid email."
**Good:** "Please enter a valid email address."

**Bad:** "An error occurred."
**Good:** "Something went wrong. Please try again, or refresh the page."

### 6.4 Buttons & Actions
Use verbs that describe the action. Avoid vague "Submit" or "OK."

| Generic | Better |
|---|---|
| Submit | Post question, Send message, Request connection |
| OK | Got it, Continue, Done |
| Cancel | Cancel, Discard changes (be specific when possible) |
| Save | Save changes, Save profile |

## 7. Communication Styles

### 7.1 In-App Notifications
Brief, factual, actionable.

**Good:** *"Aarav (1st year CSE) accepted your answer."*
**Not:** *"Great news! Aarav appreciated your contribution!"*

### 7.2 Email Communications
Slightly more formal than in-app, but still warm.

- Subject lines: clear, specific, no clickbait
- Greeting: "Hi [Name]," (informal but respectful)
- Body: get to the point in the first paragraph
- Closing: "— The Ascend team" or simply no signature for transactional emails

### 7.3 Onboarding Tour Copy
Gentle, encouraging, and brief. Each step is one sentence + (optional) supporting line.

**Step 1:** "This is where questions live. Ask anything you need help with."
**Step 2:** "And this is where stories get shared. Internships, projects, journeys."
**Step 3:** "Your reputation builds as you contribute. Earn it through helpful answers and meaningful connections."
**Step 4:** "Connect with seniors and alumni for one-on-one mentorship on specific topics."
**Step 5:** "That's the gist. You can revisit this from the Help menu anytime."

### 7.4 Community Guidelines (Tone)
Direct and respectful. State the rule, explain why briefly, no preaching.

*"Don't post personal attacks. Critique ideas, not people. Posts targeting individuals will be removed."*

Not: *"Please respect the diverse perspectives of our community members and engage in constructive dialogue."* (corporate-flavored, low signal)

### 7.5 Verification Communications
Honest about timing. Respectful regardless of outcome.

**Approval:** *"Welcome to Ascend, [Name]. Your alumni account is verified. You have full access to the platform."*

**Rejection:** *"We were unable to verify your alumni status from the information provided. Specifically: [reason]. You can re-submit with additional information, or email [admin] directly."*

## 8. Brand Application Examples

### 8.1 Login Page
- Clean layout, generous whitespace
- Maroon header with "Ascend" wordmark
- Two clear paths: "Log in" and "Sign up"
- Tagline beneath: *"For the RSET community."*

### 8.2 Question Detail Page
- Title prominent at top
- Asker's persona badge visible (or "Anonymous Student — CSE 2nd year" for anonymous)
- Body in comfortable reading width
- Answers below, accepted answer pinned with subtle Gold accent
- Faculty Endorsed answers show a Gold ribbon
- Action affordances (vote, comment, accept) clear but not visually loud

### 8.3 Profile Page
- Persona badge as the visual anchor
- Class of [Year] alongside persona for alumni
- Expertise Score visible but not hyped
- Badges displayed prominently in a tidy grid
- Activity history clean and readable

### 8.4 Empty Q&A in a Tag
*"No questions yet in [Tag]. Be the first to ask."*
With a clear "Ask a question" call-to-action.

## 9. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 9.1 Engagement Manipulation
- Don't use red dots or alarming notification badges to drive return visits
- Don't gamify in ways that feel hollow
- Don't surface "trending" content artificially
- Don't push for engagement; the value should pull users back

### 9.2 Dark Patterns
- No pre-checked "I agree" boxes
- No buried unsubscribe links
- No countdown timers creating false urgency
- No requesting permissions before they're contextually justified
- No making delete-account intentionally hard

### 9.3 Tone Failures
- Don't be condescending ("Don't worry! It's easy!")
- Don't be over-familiar ("Hey there, friend!")
- Don't be coldly transactional ("Request submitted. Reference number: #4827.")
- Don't apologize excessively in non-error contexts

### 9.4 Visual Failures
- Don't overuse Maroon to the point of overwhelming
- Don't use Gold for non-celebratory contexts
- Don't sacrifice contrast for aesthetics
- Don't use stock illustrations that feel generic

---

## 10. Living Document

These guidelines will evolve. As Ascend grows, copy patterns emerge, edge cases appear, and visual decisions get refined. Update this document as the platform matures.

The goal is consistency that feels intentional — not rigid. When in doubt, ask: *would a thoughtful senior at RSET say this to a junior they care about?* That's the test.
